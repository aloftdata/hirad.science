The **5th International Radar Aeroecology Conference (IRAC 2026)** will be held in Amsterdam on June 24-26, 2026. Furthermore there is a **radar aeroecology course** before the conference on June 22-23. The conference will be organized at [Amsterdam Science Park](https://www.google.com/maps/place/Science+Park,+Amsterdam/@52.3538274,4.9546474,17z/).

## Travel information

Amsterdam is well connected either through international trains to Europe, or by plane through Schiphol Airport. 

### Route from Schiphol Airport

**By train (recommended)**

The easiest way to travel from the airport to Amsterdam Science Park is
by train. The journey typically takes 30--40 minutes. Take a train to
*Amsterdam Centraal Station*.
At Amsterdam Centraal, transfer to a Sprinter train in the direction of
*Almere Oostvaarders* or *Lelystad Centrum*. Get off at Amsterdam
Science Park railway station.
From the station, it is a short walk (5--10 minutes) to the buildings at
Amsterdam Science Park

__Train tickets__

In some cases you are able to [directly
pay](https://www.ns.nl/en/travel-information/check-in-check-out/debit-card)
with your debit card at the entrance gates to the train station.
Alternatively, train tickets can be purchased from the NS train tickets
machines at train station (main hall airport) (with coins or ATM/Credit
cards) or online. Note that the difference between first and second
class travel in trains is negligible, although first class could be more
comfortable during rush hour, when trains tend to be overcrowded. There
is a high penalty for travelling on the train without a valid ticket.
Travelling by train within the Netherlands:
[www.ns.nl](http://www.ns.nl/).


**By taxi**

Taxis are waiting outside the arrival hall at Schiphol Airport and will
bring you to Amsterdam City Center or Amsterdam Science Park in less
than 25 minutes.

### Amsterdam Public Transport System

Although the city centre's compact size means that most areas and sights
are within walking distance of each other, Amsterdam's public transport
network (called \'GVB\') is efficient, reliable, inexpensive and widely
used. One million passengers travel the city by tram, bus, underground
metro line and ferry every day. The GVB day or multi-day ticket provides
you with unlimited travel throughout Amsterdam, both day and night, on
the bus, tram, and metro, for the number of days that best fits your
plans. 
Please note public transport can only be paid by card (no cash).

Useful websites to plan your trips within Amsterdam, the Netherlands:

- <https://www.gvb.nl/en>
- <https://9292.nl/en> (general planner, train, metro, tram, bus)
- <https://www.ns.nl/en> (train only)

## About Amsterdam

surprising faces. No other city mixes cosmopolitan style and relaxed

Amsterdam is an ideal conference destination, hosting hundreds of
world-class congresses, conventions, events and trade fairs each year.

favorite for meeting planners and delegates alike and, as such, is the
